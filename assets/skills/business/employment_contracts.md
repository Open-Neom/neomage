# Employment Contracts

Generate employment contracts, offer letters, and HR policy documents with jurisdiction-aware compliance requirements.

- Employment contracts must specify: role, compensation, benefits, termination clauses, IP assignment, confidentiality, and non-compete terms
- Offer letters are lighter than contracts: role, start date, salary, reporting structure, at-will language (US)
- Non-compete enforceability varies dramatically by jurisdiction — some states ban them entirely
- IP assignment clauses should clearly define work product ownership and pre-existing IP carve-outs
- Employee handbooks cover PTO policies, code of conduct, anti-harassment, expense reimbursement, and remote work terms
- Confidentiality/NDA sections must define what constitutes confidential information with reasonable scope and duration
- Always include a disclaimer that generated documents are templates, not legal advice — review by counsel is required
