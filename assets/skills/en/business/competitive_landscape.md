# Competitive Landscape

Competitive analysis using strategic frameworks to map market positioning, identify threats, and find differentiation opportunities.

- Porter's Five Forces assesses industry attractiveness: supplier power, buyer power, threat of substitutes, threat of new entrants, competitive rivalry
- Competitive positioning maps plot rivals on two key dimensions to reveal underserved market gaps
- Differentiation strategies: cost leadership, differentiation, or focus — trying all three simultaneously fails
- Competitive intelligence gathering covers pricing, features, messaging, hiring patterns, and technology choices
- SWOT analysis connects internal strengths/weaknesses to external opportunities/threats for actionable strategy
- Market positioning requires choosing what NOT to compete on as much as what to lead with
- Track competitor movements over time; point-in-time snapshots miss strategic direction shifts
