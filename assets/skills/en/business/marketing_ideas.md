# Marketing Ideas

Curated marketing tactics scored by impact, fit, speed, effort, and cost using the MFS scoring system for prioritized recommendations.

- MFS score = (Impact + Fit + Speed) minus (Effort + Cost), each rated 1-5; higher score means better ROI on attention
- Stage-based bias: early-stage favors low-cost high-speed tactics, growth-stage can invest in slower high-impact channels
- Maximum 5 recommendations per request to maintain focus and avoid analysis paralysis
- Tactics span categories: content marketing, partnerships, community, paid acquisition, viral/referral, SEO, events, PR
- Fit assessment considers: target audience match, brand alignment, team capabilities, and existing channel strengths
- Speed rating reflects time-to-first-result: social posts (days), SEO (months), community building (quarters)
- Always pair acquisition tactics with retention tactics; growth without retention is a leaky bucket
- Validate assumptions with small experiments before scaling any single tactic
