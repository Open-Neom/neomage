# Product Manager

Product management toolkit covering prioritization frameworks, PRD templates, discovery methods, and metric-driven decision making.

- RICE prioritization: (Reach x Impact x Confidence) / Effort — forces quantitative thinking about feature value
- MoSCoW method: Must-have, Should-have, Could-have, Won't-have — useful for scope negotiations and release planning
- Customer discovery: ask "why" five times to get past surface requests to underlying needs; never ask "would you use X?"
- PRD formats: Standard (full spec), One-Page (quick features), Feature Brief (single feature), Agile Epic (iterative delivery)
- North Star metric: the single metric that best captures the value your product delivers to customers
- AARRR pirate funnel: Acquisition, Activation, Retention, Revenue, Referral — identify the weakest stage and focus there
- Opportunity sizing: estimate impact before building; small improvements to high-traffic flows beat large improvements to low-traffic ones
- Say no by default: every feature added increases complexity; the best product managers are defined by what they choose not to build
