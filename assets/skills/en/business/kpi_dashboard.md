# KPI Dashboard

Design KPI frameworks and dashboard layouts with metrics tailored by department and organizational level.

- KPI hierarchy: Strategic (CEO/board, quarterly), Tactical (department heads, monthly), Operational (teams, weekly/daily)
- SMART KPIs are Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound — vague metrics drive vague results
- Department KPIs: Sales (pipeline velocity, win rate, ACV), Marketing (CAC, MQL-to-SQL, ROAS), Product (adoption, NPS, feature usage), Finance (burn rate, runway, gross margin)
- Dashboard layout: top row for 3-5 headline metrics, middle for trend charts, bottom for detailed breakdowns
- Leading indicators (pipeline, engagement) predict outcomes; lagging indicators (revenue, churn) confirm them
- Every KPI needs an owner, a target, a data source, and a review cadence to be actionable
- Avoid dashboard overload: 5-7 metrics per view maximum; use drill-down for detail
- Red/yellow/green status indicators require clearly defined thresholds agreed upon in advance
