# Pricing Strategy

Value-based pricing frameworks covering packaging, value metrics, price levels, and tier design for SaaS and digital products.

- Three pricing decisions: packaging (what's in each tier), value metric (what you charge per), price level (how much)
- Value-based pricing: price relative to customer value received, not cost to deliver — requires understanding willingness-to-pay
- Van Westendorp method: survey asking "too cheap / cheap / expensive / too expensive" to find acceptable price range
- Good/Better/Best tier design: Good captures price-sensitive users, Better is the target tier (most popular), Best captures high-value customers
- Value metric selection: choose a metric that scales with customer value (seats, usage, revenue) — flat pricing leaves money on the table
- Freemium vs free trial: freemium works for viral products with low marginal cost; free trial works when value requires setup investment
- Price anchoring: show the highest tier first to make the middle tier feel reasonable
- Anti-patterns: cost-plus pricing ignores value, competitor-matching ignores differentiation, discounting trains customers to wait
