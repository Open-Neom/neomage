# Launch Strategy

Product launch planning using the ORB framework across five progressive phases from internal testing to full public release.

- ORB framework categorizes channels: Owned (blog, email, social), Rented (Product Hunt, marketplaces, ads), Borrowed (press, influencers, partnerships)
- Five launch phases: Internal (team testing) -> Alpha (trusted users) -> Beta (wider audience) -> Early Access (waitlist) -> Full Launch
- Each phase has specific goals: Internal validates core flow, Alpha finds critical bugs, Beta tests scalability, Early Access builds momentum
- Product Hunt strategy: launch Tuesday-Thursday, prepare assets in advance, rally an engaged community, respond to every comment
- Pre-launch checklist: landing page, email capture, social proof, demo/video, press kit, support documentation
- Post-launch momentum requires a 30-day content calendar, follow-up sequences, and community engagement plan
- Measure launch success by activation rate and retention, not just sign-ups or press mentions
- Anti-pattern: launching without a defined audience or distribution channel leads to zero traction regardless of product quality
